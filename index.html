About

Catherine is currently a junior at Stanford University Online High School, an active member of the Math Club and FTC Robotics Team 5206. Catherine was awarded the Spirit of Ramanujan Math Initiative Fellowship in 2017 and will be doing number theory research in the summer. Passionate to use technology to improve education, she has interned at Khan Academy and had created multiple educational apps, receiving a scholarship to Apple’s WWDC in 2016. Catherine is also an AI enthusiast, 1 of 24 attendees worldwide accepted to SAILORS (Stanford’s Artificial Intelligence Lab Summer Outreach Program) in 2015. An NCWIT Affiliate Award Winner and She++ #include Fellow, Catherine co-founded and directs PixelHacks (the Bay Area’s first all-female high school hackathon) and heads KA’s Student Advisory Council. Outside of school, she runs her own AP Calculus classes, mentors the San Jose Math Circle, swims competitively, and is an avid member of #DubNation.

Saluton

Saluton is a chatbot that helps individuals learn foreign languages through conversational learning; it is deployed on Facebook messenger and coded using Javascript, Gupshup, and Google Translate's API (along with some application of natural language processing). Won Second Place Overall at HSHacks, the largest high school hackathon in the world.

  • Date: March 2017

Letter Pop

Letter Pop is an iOS and Android word game, in which there are letters on balloons and you pop the balloons to spell out words. The goal of this game is to improve users' spelling and vocabulary.

  • Project: Letter Pop
  • Date: May 2014 - June 2016
  • Code: Github repo

Visual Cryptography

This project illustrates the concept of visual cryptography (as designed by Moni Naor and Adi Shamir) using 2-bit and 3-bit encryption programs. A photo consisting of only black and white pixels (two bits) is inputted into the program, and two images of seemingly random noise will be generated. The two images could be layered to create (“decrypt”) the original inputted image using XOR. Written in Java.

  • Project: Visual Cryptography
  • Date: May 2016
  • Code: Github Repo
