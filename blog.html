Summer@Stanford

August 9-22, 2017

At my second Summer@Stanford, I participated in the Physics Lab program, where we studied and performed experiments in all physics topics, including waves, dark matter, magnetism, thermodynamics, projectile motion, and Maxwell's Equations. For our self-designed project, our group created a motor and Arduino photogate to measure how the angular speed of the motor varies with change in current. I also met and interacted with numerous new and returning OHS students.

Emory REU

June 5 - July 15, 2017

I attended a number theory and algebraic geometry REU (Research Experience for Undergraduates) at Emory University, led by Professor Ken Ono; the six-week research program was sponsored and paid by the Spirit of Ramanujan Math Talent Initiative Fellowship. Our research, which revolved around finding and proving that an infinite class of polynomials satisfies the Riemann Hypothesis, culminated in this paper.

She++ #include Fellowship Summit

April 5-7, 2017

The She++ #include Fellowship Summit was an all-expense paid three-day trip in the Silicon Valley honoring 30 high school Fellows (out of 500+ applicants) who had created and led an initiative to encourage diversity in the technology field. I had the chance to present PixelHacks, as well as meet many mentors from Stanford University and industry professionals from numerous tech companies. We also visited and toured Google, Facebook, Accel, GoDaddy, and Groupon.

HSHacks III

March 25-26, 2017

At HSHacks, the largest high school hackathon in the world, my teammates and I won second place overall from building Saluton. Saluton is a chatbot that helps individuals learn foreign languages through conversational learning; it is deployed on Facebook messenger and coded using Javascript, Gupshup, and Google Translate's API (along with some application of natural language processing). I had a lot of fun at HSHacks, especially in learning and figuring out how to call Google Translate's API to our code.

PixelHacks

January 28-29, 2017

PixelHacks Team and Winners

PixelHacks, the Bay Area's first all-female high school hackathon, was founded on a mission to increase the presence & active participation of women in the tech field and was open to aspiring female programmers of all levels & experience. As the co-founder & director of PixelHacks, I spent 5 months to plan the event - securing sponsors, finding a venue, marketing, setting up workshops, recruiting judges & mentors, prepare swag & prizes, etc. All attendees had 24 hours to create a software project, with the final submissions ranging from helping autistic children to climate change to education. The two-day event was a great success and I was really happy to see the positive responses of all the attendees!

Arduino

Summer 2016

I took part in IvyCube's Introduction to Robotics course, in which I built and programmed many projects that involved LEDs, buttons, musical speakers, joysticks, etc using Arduino. My final project, built with Ariel B., was a line-following car. Employing multiple color sensors and a complicated detection algorithm, our car was able to successfully track down and follow a line on the ground in any direction. WWDC

Selfie with Tim Cook

SAILORS

August 2015

Photo via Wired

SAILORS, Stanford's Artificial Intelligence Laboratory Outreach Summer Program, is a two-week program held at Stanford that provided both broad exposure to AI topics through faculty lectures and industry field trips, as well as an in-depth experience with a research area through hands-on projects. As 1 of 24 students selected worldwide, I got to hear from AI professors from Stanford and Berkeley, perform hands-on tutorials implementing machine learning concepts, and engaged in a project that utilized natural-language processing to aid disaster relief, specifically categorizing tweets from Hurricane Sandy into those asking for help and those providing help.

MathPath

Summer 2015

MathPath is a premier four-week residential summer program for "students showing high promise and interest in mathematics." Over the four weeks in Portland, Oregon, I took classes in Eisenstein numbers, Boolean algebra, Poisson distribution, partitions, etc. Furthermore, I attended seminars led by renowned mathematicians including John Conway and Paul Zeitz. It was an amazing experience that truly inspired me to delve deeper into mathematical problem solving and proofs.

Economics Challenge State Championships

April 7, 2015

Team PikaSquad

Our team of four high school freshmen had competed in the online preliminary round of the National Economics Challenge and qualified for the State Championships by placing in the top 5 of Northern California. We competed with other brilliant (and definitely older) teams at the Federal Reserve Bank of San Francisco, where we were tested on our knowledge of microeconomics, macro-economics, and current events, as well as our buzzer pressing skills in exhilarating quiz bowl-styled rounds!
