She++ #include Fellowship Summit

April 5-7, 2017

The She++ #include Fellowship Summit was an all-expense paid three-day trip in the Silicon Valley honoring 30 high school Fellows (out of 500+ applicants) who had created and led an initiative to encourage diversity in the technology field. I had the chance to present PixelHacks, as well as meet many mentors from Stanford University and industry professionals from numerous tech companies. We also visited and toured Google, Facebook, Accel, GoDaddy, and Groupon.

HSHacks III

March 25-26, 2017

At HSHacks, the largest high school hackathon in the world, my teammates and I won second place overall from building Saluton. Saluton is a chatbot that helps individuals learn foreign languages through conversational learning; it is deployed on Facebook messenger and coded using Javascript, Gupshup, and Google Translate's API (along with some application of natural language processing). I had a lot of fun at HSHacks, especially in learning and figuring out how to call Google Translate's API to our code.

PixelHacks

January 28-29, 2017

PixelHacks Team and Winners

PixelHacks, the Bay Area's first all-female high school hackathon, was founded on a mission to increase the presence & active participation of women in the tech field and was open to aspiring female programmers of all levels & experience. As the co-founder & director of PixelHacks, I spent 5 months to plan the event - securing sponsors, finding a venue, marketing, setting up workshops, recruiting judges & mentors, prepare swag & prizes, etc. All attendees had 24 hours to create a software project, with the final submissions ranging from helping autistic children to climate change to education. The two-day event was a great success and I was really happy to see the positive responses of all the attendees!

Arduino

Summer 2016

I took part in IvyCube's Introduction to Robotics course, in which I built and programmed many projects that involved LEDs, buttons, musical speakers, joysticks, etc using Arduino. My final project, built with Ariel B., was a line-following car. Employing multiple color sensors and a complicated detection algorithm, our car was able to successfully track down and follow a line on the ground in any direction. WWDC

June 13-17, 2016

Selfie with Tim Cook

As 1 of 350 WWDC Scholars who Apple selected across the world, I had the honor of attending this year's Worldwide Developers Conference (WWDC) in San Francisco. I engaged in a week of numerous seminars, keynotes, and hands-on labs held by Apple engineers, during which I improved my existing iOS apps and learned how to implement new software that Apple had just released (all still in beta versions). I also had the fortune of meeting Craig Federighi, Apple's senior vice president, and Tim Cook! As I held up my Android phone and asked him if he would be willing to take a selfie with me, he said to me, "You need to upgrade your phone..."
